# Making Synthesis Tutor More Revolutionary in EdTech

*Saved from conversation — March 2025*

This document summarizes research-backed ways to make the app more "revolutionary" for the edtech space and what the education space would benefit from. The app is a **K–5 math tutor for families** (conversational tutor + manipulatives, one deep fraction-equivalence lesson + generic MCQ engine, parent dashboard, no school/LMS integrations).

---

## 1. Evidence-Based Learning Engine (Spaced + Interleaved + Mastery)

**Why it's revolutionary:** Most consumer math apps are either one-off practice or linear "next lesson" flows. Few combine **spaced practice**, **interleaved practice**, and **mastery** in one engine.

- **Spaced practice:** Meta-analyses show a small–medium effect (e.g. g ≈ 0.28) for math; effects are larger when material is clearly isolated (g ≈ 0.43). Spaced recall reduces forgetting months later.
- **Interleaving:** Mixing problem types (e.g. equivalence, comparison, addition) beats blocked practice (e.g. 61% vs 38% in one RCT, d ≈ 0.83).
- **Mastery thresholds:** Higher mastery bars before "moving on" reduce forgetting; many apps move on too quickly.

**What would benefit the app:** A **learning engine** that: (1) schedules **review** of past topics (spaced repetition), (2) **interleaves** different fraction/subject skills in practice, and (3) only marks a lesson "complete" or unlocks the next when a **mastery threshold** is met (e.g. correct on multiple days or in mixed sets).

---

## 2. Real Diagnostic + Truly Adaptive Paths (Not Just One Lesson Deep)

**Why it's revolutionary:** Parents and schools want **diagnosis of level and gaps** in minutes, then **paths that adapt** to those gaps. Adaptive systems are reported to improve outcomes (e.g. ~42% in some studies); 71% of institutions are expected to use adaptive platforms by 2026.

**What would benefit the app:**
- **Short diagnostic (5–10 min):** A small set of tasks (including with existing visuals: number line, array, fraction bar) that estimates level and **misconceptions**.
- **Adaptive sequencing:** Use diagnostic + ongoing performance to **skip or fast-track** lessons, and **generate or choose** practice that targets specific gaps.
- **Misconception-aware tutor:** Extend the declarative script so the tutor's hints and next steps are driven by **identified misconceptions** (from wrong answers or patterns), not only correct/incorrect.

---

## 3. Parent Dashboard That Explains *Why* and *What to Do* (Not Just "Progress")

**Why it's revolutionary:** Parents want **transparent, interpretable** progress: mastery, streaks, and—critically—**what's going wrong and what to do**.

**What would benefit the app:**
- **Activity timeline that's meaningful:** e.g. "Completed Equivalent Fractions Explore," "Struggled on Practice 2 (1/3 equivalence), then got it," "Review: fraction comparison (spaced)."
- **Simple "why" summaries:** e.g. "Strong on splitting and shading; still mixing up which number is numerator when reading fractions."
- **Actionable nudges:** e.g. "This week: one 10-min review of equivalence" or "Ready for comparing fractions."
- **Optional weekly digest:** Email or in-app summary with 2–3 bullets (wins + one focus area).

---

## 4. Accessibility and Inclusion (UDL + Math-Specific)

**Why it's revolutionary:** A large share of educational sites remain inaccessible; math is especially hard (notation, diagrams, manipulatives). **Universal Design for Learning (UDL)** and math-specific accessibility are still rare in consumer math tutors.

**What would benefit the app:**
- **Multiple representations:** Same concept via **text, image, speech** (e.g. fraction bar + "one half" + optional read-aloud for prompts and feedback).
- **Multiple input modes:** Besides tap/drag, support **voice** ("shade one half") or **keyboard** where it makes sense.
- **Perceivable math:** For any equation or key visual, **alt text / descriptions** that work with screen readers.
- **Dyscalculia-friendly design:** Clear number formatting, minimal clutter, consistent layout, optional larger numerals and high contrast.

---

## 5. Teacher/School Bridge (Without Rebuilding the Product)

**Why it matters:** EdTech surveys show **integration pain** (e.g. LMS not playing well with apps) and **time spent on admin** as top pain points.

**What would benefit the app:**
- **Class/school links:** Teacher creates a "class" and shares a code/link; parents add their child. Teacher sees **aggregate or per-student** progress.
- **Lightweight integrations:** Single Sign-On (e.g. Google), or roster import, so schools can assign the app as homework without new logins.
- **Export or share:** "Download progress report (PDF)" or "Share with teacher" so the rich parent view can inform the teacher.

---

## 6. Engagement That's About Thinking, Not Rewards

**What would benefit the app:**
- **Explain-back prompts:** e.g. "Why is that equivalent?" (voice or short text) before marking correct.
- **Error as learning:** After a wrong answer, script the tutor to **name the misconception** in simple language and give one more try with a hint.
- **Minimal gamification:** Streaks and progress are fine; avoid extrinsic rewards that overshadow the "I figured it out" moment. Position as "thinking coach," not "game with math."

---

## 7. Standards Alignment and Credibility

**What would benefit the app:** Explicitly tag lessons and units to **Common Core / state standards**. Optional "Standards view" in the parent or teacher dashboard. A short "How it works" or "Research" page (spaced practice, mastery, UDL) would strengthen trust.

---

## Summary: What the Ed Space Would Benefit From

| Area | What the ed space needs | How the app could deliver |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Learning science** | Spaced + interleaved + mastery, not just "next lesson" | Scheduling engine + mastery thresholds + mixed review |
| **Personalization** | Real diagnosis and adaptation, not one path for all | Short diagnostic, misconception detection, adaptive sequencing |
| **Parent–school link** | One view of the child, less fragmentation | Rich parent dashboard + optional class link + export for teachers |
| **Accessibility** | Math that works for more learners (UDL, dyscalculia, screen reader) | Multi-modal representation/expression + accessible manipulatives |
| **Engagement** | Deep engagement (reasoning, explaining) over shallow rewards | Explain-back, misconception-focused feedback, "thinking coach" positioning |
| **Trust** | Standards + research-backed design | Standards tags + clear, honest "research" narrative |
